## Engineering Philosophy

> *"A UAV that works in MATLAB is a simulation. A UAV that works in wind, rain, and EMI interference is an engineering achievement."*

Control theory without hardware awareness is academic fiction. My work is driven by three constraints that simulators ignore:

**SWaP-C** â€” Every gram of compute added is a gram of flight time lost. Every watt consumed is a meter of range sacrificed. Design decisions are engineering tradeoffs, not feature lists.

**Sensor Noise Reality** â€” A Kalman Filter assumes Gaussian noise. Real IMUs produce bias drift, vibration spikes, and magnetic interference. The gap between the textbook and the bench is where most systems fail.

**Latency Budgets** â€” A 100ms control loop on a multirotor at full throttle is the difference between recovery and a crash. Real-time constraints aren't a software problem â€” they're a system architecture problem.
